The Problem

Americans rely on public health for a multitude of resources. From preventing disease outbreak and injury, monitering health status, and providing medical expertise. Yet, after the Covid-19 pandemic, it is easy to see that public health has been chronically underfunded for the past few years. It is estimated that state and local health depatements need to hire 80,000 more full-time equivalent positions (FTEs) — an increase of nearly 80% — to provide adequate infrastructure and a minimum package of public health services.(Debeaumont)


It will be a large task getting these resources acquired, but entirely possible. On the other pages we detail the individual steps needed to accomplish this lofty goal. It is clear increased funding is needed, but it will also be important to make health careers a more favorable proffesion. Thus we have the steps seperated between soulutions involving funding and solutions focused on workers.
